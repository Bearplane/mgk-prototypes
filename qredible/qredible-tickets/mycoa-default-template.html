1The Lab Report

A lab report from a reputable laboratory will show the name, address, phone number, and any certifications earned. The COA attests to the report's validity and is signed by a senior staff member.

2The Date

Make sure the date is recent. When purchasing CBD, the date on the COA should be under one year.

3The Lot or Batch Identifier

Be sure the lot or batch on the COA matches the lot or batch on the product label, box, or sticker on the bottom of the container.

4Product Name

Confirm that the product name on the packaging matches the product name on the COA.

5THC

Delta-9-tetrahydrocannabinol (THC) is the primary psychoactive cannabinoid in the cannabis plant. By federal law, THC in CBD must be 0.3% or under to be sold in the United States and Canada.

6CBD Spectrum

CBD is commonly called full spectrum CBD. When THC is present in CBD, it creates the entourage effect. You may also wish to purchase THC-free (broad spectrum) CBD, which has non-detectable amounts of THC.

7Residual Testing

A reputable COA will report residual testing for pesticides, heavy metals, microbials, mycotoxins, and residual solvents. Every category should show a "PASS" result to confirm product safety.
