Policy Lifecycle — Customer Flow & Chaining

How a policy gets onto a merchant, how it shapes what the platform does for that merchant, and how it changes over time. Companion to Policy Editor (authoring), Bulletin Authoring (publishing), and Policy Evidence Loop (closed-loop detection).

1 The Mental Model

A policy is a versioned JSON doc covering merchant tiers, checklist, product classification, MRI config, payment rails, scoring, reports, and enforcement. A merchant subscribes to one or more policies. Everything the platform does for that merchant — scans, scoring, dashboard, reports — is computed from their active policies. Merchants don't fork policies; per-merchant exemptions live as overlays on top of canonical ones.

5 Policy Chaining 🧩 — Lightweight, Specific, Composable

Instead of one giant policy per merchant, the model favors small, narrow, versioned policies that stack. A Texas hemp merchant who takes MasterCard chains three policies; a multi-category merchant might chain four or more.

Example A — 3-layer stack (Acme Hemp Co)

hemp-cbd-base-v3
Federal hemp/CBD baseline. COA requirements, FDA disclaimers, prohibited-state shipping rules, total Δ9-THC ≤ 0.3% threshold.
state-overlay-tx-v2
Texas-specific. TX hemp manufacturer registration check, quarterly bank statement review, TX-specific labeling rules.
payment-rails-mc-v1
MasterCard processor requirements. Authenticated scanning, MID tracking, quarterly compliance report export.

Example B — 4-layer stack (multi-category merchant)

hemp-cbd-base-v3
Federal hemp/CBD baseline.
kratom-watchlist-v1
Adds 7-OH and mitragynine watchlist, state-by-state prohibition map, age gate enforcement.
peptides-research-only-v1
Research-only disclaimer enforcement, B2B buyer verification, prohibited consumer rails (Venmo/Cashapp).
payment-rails-visa-v1
Visa-specific monitoring requirements.
OLD model — industry-keyed

One industry row → one giant rules table that conflated everything. Adding a state rule meant editing the master table and risking regressions everywhere.

NEW model — chained policies

Small, named, versioned, narrow policies. Each one has one job. A merchant's compliance posture is a thin stack of these, and changes are scoped to whichever layer needs to move.

7 Overlay Model

⚠️ PROPOSED — STATUS: TBD. The Policy Editor mockup currently authors canonical policies only. There is no overlay surface yet. This section is the proposed shape.

Merchants legitimately need exceptions sometimes — a single SKU exempted because it's wholesale-only, a check disabled with justification, a custom keyword the scanner should ignore for them. Two ways to handle that:

  • Overlay (recommended): A thin per-merchant doc that layers on top of the base policies, listing exemptions and additions. Engine evaluates policy + overlay. Auditable, scoped, easy to roll back.
  • Variant policies (avoid): Forking the canonical policy into a per-merchant variant. Cleanly versionable but explodes the policy library and detaches the merchant from upstream updates.

Worked Example

Base policy hemp-cbd-base-v3 says: "All CBD products must show batch number on label." Acme Hemp Co requests an exemption for a single wholesale-only SKU not sold to consumers. Analyst grants overlay acme-hemp-co-overlays-v1 with one entry exempting that SKU from that check. Engine now evaluates policy + overlay for Acme.

What Acme sees in their portal: "Exempted for you: 1 product (SKU SP-027). Reason: wholesale-only SKU, not sold to consumers. Granted by: J. Floyd, 2026-04-15. Expires: never."
❓
Open question — Overlays in v1?Do per-merchant exemptions exist as overlays on top of canonical policies, or do we punt on exemptions entirely in v1 and route them all to support tickets? Worth deciding before the first non-trivial customer goes live.
