📐 Design Note — Match Confidence & Actions: The sanctions screening API returns fuzzy match scores using phonetic algorithms (Soundex, Metaphone), Levenshtein distance, and field cross-referencing. ≥95% → HIGH confidence, flagged and shown first. 80–94% → MEDIUM confidence. <80% → filtered out (not surfaced to reviewers).

"✓ Clear — False Positive" marks the specific match as reviewed and confirmed not a true SDN hit. This creates an immutable audit trail entry with the reviewer's name, timestamp, and rationale (via note). The contact's overall sanctions status remains "Review Required" until ALL flagged matches on that contact are cleared or escalated.

"🚨 Escalate to Compliance Review": (1) Contact status → ESCALATED (red badge). (2) An internal Qredible compliance task is created — not visible to the merchant. (3) Qredible compliance team reviews the match. (4) Outcome: Clear (false positive confirmed → status returns to Clear), Request Documents (creates KYC compliance case sent to merchant → status → Case Open), or Confirmed Match (processor alerted, merchant suspended). Once escalated, the match is locked from further edits by the processor pending compliance resolution.

New Field Why It's Needed
Merchant ID Links uploaded contact records to existing company entities in Q-Trust. Without this, records may create duplicate companies or fail to associate correctly with the right merchant account.
Owner #1–2 Phone Phone number provides an additional identity signal for sanctions screening. Also required for direct outreach during compliance review or escalation.
Owner #1 Role / Title Role (e.g., "Owner", "CFO") determines whether a person qualifies as a Control Person for OFAC screening prioritization. Title provides additional context for compliance officers during review.
Owner #1–2 Beneficial Owner (Y/N) Flags individuals with 25%+ ownership or control. FinCEN's CDD Rule requires financial institutions to identify and verify beneficial owners. This flag drives screening priority — beneficial owners are screened first.
Owner #1–2 Control Person (Y/N) Identifies individuals with significant management or operational control (CEO, CFO, COO, GP). FinCEN requires at least one control person per legal entity. Control persons are always screened regardless of ownership %, even at 0% equity.
Owner #1 DL State / Number / Expiry Driver's license data dramatically improves enhanced identity matching accuracy. Name + DOB alone produces high false-positive rates for common names (e.g., "Michael Johnson"). Adding state-issued ID cross-reference reduces false positives by ~40% and increases match confidence scores.
Contact Phone / Role Enables contact-level OFAC screening for non-owner team members who may still be classified as control persons. Role is needed to determine screening necessity and priority tier.
📐 Design Note — Template v2 Compatibility: The updated template is backward-compatible — existing required fields are unchanged. New fields are optional in the upload parser but will trigger a soft warning if Beneficial Owner or Control Person flags are missing for Owner records (since these are required for accurate OFAC screening). Uploads without DL fields will succeed but contacts will be flagged as "Screened — Low Confidence" rather than "Clear", prompting operators to collect and upload the missing DL data for a full re-screen.
